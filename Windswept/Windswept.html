Marc Rozendaal

February - March 2023

This project took place during Project Context II, where we got an assignment from an external client. In this case that client was TNO, who asked us to create a game about the communication between energy companies and the community about placing windmills and solar farms in their community. The goal of this game was to show the higher-ups of these energy companies the importance of an open conversation between the company and the community.

This was a difficult project, since we couldn't agree on an approach with the client. So after a number of iterations we settled on a game where you are the one responsible for placing the windmills, but you don't know whether the community will be okay with your placement until you talk to them.

During this project I was the sole developer. I spent most of my time working on all the gameplay systems making the game work. One of the things I built was a level editor, which tied into almost all systems within the game. The level editor allowed our game designer to not only design the level, but also place the people on the level, and design the approval rates for every tile. With the game taking place on an hexagonal grid, I had to use a different coordinate system. This challenged my math and programming skills.

Windswept was made in Unity.
